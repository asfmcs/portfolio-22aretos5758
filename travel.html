HISTORY OF ALASKA

Alaska is a U.S. State in the western part of Canada. It is mostly known for the beautiful sceneries it has, with mountains, forests, and glaciers. Alaska is also normally seen as a place to rest, with the dog breed of Siberiam Husky being the most recognizable. Alaska was originally from Russia, as they discovered it, Alaska meaning the Great Land. This was until the U.S. bought Alaska from Russia, becoming known as the biggest state of the U.S.

LANDMARKS

The Denali Mountain

A Mountain 6,000 feet above sea level which more than a thousand people try to climb every year, with only half succeeding. The Denali Mountain's original name was Bolshaya Gora, meaning Denali in Russian, but it was changed in 1880 till 1896 into Densmore's Mountain. In 1896 it was changed to Mount McKinley to support William McKinley, who was aa presidential Candidate at that time. The name finally got back to Denali in 2015, when president Barrack Obama announced it was getting its original name back. The Denali Mountain is actually the center piece of the Denali National Park and Reserve, another great reason to visit Alaska.







Aurora Borealis

A well-known aurora, also known as the Northern Lights, which can be viewed in many places. Although the best way to watch it as actually here in Alaska. The Aurora Borealis is named after two ancient gods, Aurora, the goddess of the dawn, and Boreal, the god of the northern winds. The Aurora Borealis is a phenomenom that happens when the sun lets out solar wind through sunspots, they eventually reach a place in the gravitational field of earth where charged particles lie, these two forces colliding make the amazing lightshow of the Aurora's





Glacier Bay National Park and Preserve

A huge area with glaciers and mountains. The glacier is normally passed by cruiseships and has riverside and forest trails for you to walk along and see the nature it holds Glacier Bay National Park is also the home of many species, as it is a complete sanctuary for animals like Humpack Whales, Killer Whales, Harbor Seal, Sea Otters, Bears, and tons more. It has laboratories studying the wild life in a non dangerous way. Most importantly, it has the 11 glaciers which you can visit in boats, cruiseships, and learn of their cretion in their online classes.
